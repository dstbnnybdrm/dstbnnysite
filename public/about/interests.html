music

i like to think that i have a pretty broad music taste and will probably enjoy listening to most things if they're done well, with sincerity and intention, or simply just interestingly, but of course i definitely do gravitate more towards a select few of my favourite genres/scenes:

emo

  • second wave
    • Sunny Day Real Estate
    • Mineral
  • revival
    • Modern Baseball
    • Oso Oso
    • Macseal
  • fifth wave
    • Weatherday (and anything Sputnik touches)
    • home is where
    • asian glow
    • glass beach
  • skramz (screamo)
    • Flowers Taped to Pens
    • I Wrote Haikus About Cannibalism in Your Yearbook

musically i'm very much indebted to emo of all the different waves and subgenres, i think there's at least a little bit of emo influence in all of my own work, even when i branch out into many different styles. i couldn't exactly explain why, but it's a genre and scene that is very near and dear to me; i could probably write a dissertation on emo. currently, i'm especially excited in seeing all of the creative stuff coming out of the latest fifth wave, it's all so varied and unique while keeping with the spirit of the genre.

indie rock/etc.

  • Car Seat Headrest
  • Neutral Milk Hotel
  • Slaughter Beach, Dog
  • Greg Mendez

i'm kind of just lumping these together, they don't have too much in common other than a generally lofi or distorted production, sometimes a folky/acoustic presentation, and smart, wordy, sometimes stream-of-consciousness type lyrics. there's an honesty that's present in all of them that i find very captivating.

lofi/bedroom pop/etc.

  • Laptop Funeral
  • flatsound
  • Field Medic
  • smith corona
  • bedbug
  • fuvk
  • Birdspotter

my love for cassettes and tape largely stems from my love for the intimate, textured, DIY nature of lofi music (before that label eventually became synonomous with lofi hip-hop). a lot of the artists in this vein i discovered through haphazard clicks on old Soundcloud and the now-defunct cassette label Z Tapes (whose founder now runs music blog/label Start-Track!)

hardcore/mathcore/etc.

  • 'SABELLA
  • The Beautiful Ones
  • The Dillenger Escape Plan
  • early Daughters
  • SeeYouSpaceCowboy

i was never super into the metal music that, say, my dad was into and so i thought that i was just generally not into "heavy" music. until i discovered the visceral, breakdown-laden, scream-yelling vocals type of hardcore and beat-down. something about the sheer, apparent aggression connected more to me than i guess traditional 90's era metal music. and from there, branching out into other subgenres, i fell in love with the frenetic, screeching madness of other mathcore/sasscore and the like; music that sounds like it's actively tearing itself apart.

shoegaze/noise pop/noise rock

  • Number Girl
  • Sonic Youth
  • MASS OF THE FERMENTING DREGS
  • Uchuu Nekoko
  • My Bloody Valentine
  • Jesus and the Mary Chain

maybe it seems strange to lump some of these together? but i think they generally capture similar qualities i really enjoy in music. bands like Sonic Youth and My Bloody Valentine really opened me up to using really esoteric guitar tunings and experimenting with all the different non-traditional sounds a guitar can make. and bands like Number Girl and Jesus and the Mary Chain really solidified my love for noise and distortion in their own different ways.

EDM/indietronica/hyperpop/digicore

  • EDEN
  • Porter Robinson
  • Crywolf
  • San Holo
  • Jane Remover
  • Underscores
  • saoirse dream
  • patrick hardy
  • 100 Gecs (Laura Les in particular)

my production and general musical journey starts with EDM, actually. initially i was into the kind of "gamer music" you would find in maybe some kid's youtube intro (no shade to any of those artists! a lot of them are still great!) but later i went on to seek out electronic artists that moreso explored emotional qualities in their work. and now the current wave of electronic music, starting from Soundcloud-borne artists, is really, incredibly inspiring in its abundance of creativity.

jungle/breakcore/hardcore/gabber/etc.

  • Machine Girl
  • Bogdan Raczynski
  • Venetian Snares
  • SONIC DRAGOLGO
  • Syndicate
  • Sewerslvt

(technically these count as EDM in some form? but i think they each form their own distinct communities).

jungle and adjacent genres are probably my most recent new love in music, i think from a rising appreciation i had of sample-based music and a subsequent exploration of 90's rave music, whose iconic synth sounds and sample-use i've since fallen in love with. i then discovered breakcore, admittedly, from it's recent gen Z–headed boom in online popularity (which i almost feel like should have its own distinct genre name at this point since it's arguably evolved from its initial defining traits), but i find myself leaning more towards the "controlled chaos" of breakcore from the early 2000's most days. even so, there's still a lot more that i want to explore in the genre!

    • .flow

i had some experience with RPG Maker games before but i really started to delve into them around the height of the COVID-19 pandemic. among some of the very first i played around this time was "Yume Nikki" and that was a special experience that i feel you can only really have once; just the atmosphere, the freedom and aimlessness, it all culminated into such a unique sense of comfort and escapism that i think i really needed at the time.

i got into some of the classics shortly after: games like "OFF", "ib", "Re:Kinder", etc., all with their own unique blends of horror, the surreal, and a tiny bit of tongue-in-cheek self-awareness yet with a paradoxically sobering earnestness (wow! what a pretentious string of words! lmao). there's a kind of culture or through-line you can see when you play a number of RPG Maker games that i just fell in love with so immediately; they can feel so intimate and personal—almost to the point of feeling uncomfortably close sometimes—and that's not something you necessarily get a lot anywhere else.
