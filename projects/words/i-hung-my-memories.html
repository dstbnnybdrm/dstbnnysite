i hung my memories (aug - oct 2022)

dada poems made out of fragments of many personal writings from around 2017-2020.

putting all the old writings i could find, from old phone memos to forgotten google doc pages, into a single document and randomly shuffling the words around with a generator, i try to find some kind of meaning out of the result, cutting and stitching together whole phrases and pieces of nonsense sentences.

you and i, writing on trees (sep 18, 5:28pm)

i never changed like you did. time shoved months out of me.
my radio waves break and
messages fall out.

despite this you'll
think the same words, keeping the same letters,
almost as if you and i were writing on trees.

when summer's chemicals
paint flowerbed seasons

every river (aug 21, 6:31pm)

her image waved of every river i walked,
a hope too used out.
i lit apart on why, at last,
everything about us seemed crowded.

small; i feel it but none was felt.
so, still lord, why dream?
